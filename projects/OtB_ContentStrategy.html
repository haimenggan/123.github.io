OtB Content Optimization

Content Strategy & Content Optimization

I conducted content strategy for On the Boards' website to increase
online ticket sales, subscriptions and donations.

Introduction

Content is the king and user experience is the queen. A good product includes both sides. To understand what is a good content strategy, I took a 10-weeks content strategy class to propose content strategy for On the Boards’ website. Based on the business goals and audience research provided by the professor, everyone in the class provided deliverables and content strategy individually.

To better showcase my content strategy for OtB, I would like to walk you through the content strategy for one of the client’s business goals - increase online ticket sales, subscriptions and donations.

Figure 1: Business goals and audience

Process

In the first two weeks, I started with understanding stakeholders’ perspectives, business goals and projects objectives. Then, I examined the needs, motivations, and lifestyles of the target audiences in relationship to the business in the third week. From week 4th- 8th, I examined the content and technology environments that shape the content design and provided deliverables. The deliverables include: content audit, comparative/competitive audit, content/channel map, site map and core model, page description diagram and page table. Finally, I synthesized the results of my discovery process and deliverables to deliver a content strategy addressing the client’s business goals and project objectives.

Figure 2: Project base

01 /  Content audit

In this audit, I evaluated qualitative aspects of client’s web content specifically relating to business goals, audience goals and needs, taxonomy and organization, as well as issues of quality. I used Ahava Leibtag’s heuristic set to evaluate the content. They are findable, readable, understandable, actionable, sharable. The conclusions of the audit are:

  • The OtB branding is inconsistent. The logo, images, color scheme and typography of OtB web pages are hard for the audience to recognize, which might be dicult for the audience to trust the authenticity of OtB’s communication. It is also a distraction from the core message.
  • Graphic and text are the basic formats of the contents of the OtB website. In order to attract more audience and increase subscriptions, OtB can use video, infographic, and photo gallery to showcase its performances and stories.
  • OtB can add a “share to social media” button to its web page to engage the audience and promote its performances.

Figure 3: Content audit

02 /  Comparative analysis

In this comparative analysis, I used homepage first impressions, target audience, brand messaging, product& service messaging, content topics, formats, differentiators, social media, navigation, information architecture, search, language (tone and voice), community features, and on-page content hierarchy, these heuristics to compare with five theaters' websites.

Figure 4: Comparative analysis

03 /  Content map

The content topic map is based on the axis including theme, channel, format, action, CTA(call to action) / tags.

TThe Y axis of this map is action, including inform, answer, and educate. The inform section includes all of the information the audience might need to know if the client wants to increase audience’s awareness and the number of subscriptions, ticket buyers and donation. The answer section includes the questions from the audience. The educate section is what the client wants to educate the audience after the audience read the website content.
