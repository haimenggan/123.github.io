CHID Website

Website Redesign & Design Thinking and Practice

We redesigned the website of CHID program in 10 weeks.

Background

During the spring quarter of 2018, our Ethical Design Thinking and Practice Focus Group created a new outreach portfolio for the CHID (Comparative History of Ideas) department at University of Washington using approaches from strategic communications and social media, critical university studies, ethical design thinking and design sprint methodologies. In the design print section, we shared the Sprint schedule and briefly introduce the methodologies we used during the Sprint: expert interviews, lighting decision jam, HMWs, vote on solutions, effort impact scale, identify solutions to prototype, and hive mind. We worked as a team to ideate, discover, plan, design(sketches, wireframes), prototype (high-fidelity interactive mockups), evaluate(interviews and usability testing), and iterate on our creations.

Process

01 /  Lighting decision jam

We kicked off the project with lightning decision jam methodology. After reviewing the expert interview reports, everyone in our team sat at a table and spent 7 minutes writing all the challenges, or concerns that we think CHID have faced without discussion. After this section, everyone stood up at the white board to very quickly explain each challenge as they sticked them to the surface. Nobody else in the team is allowed to speak here. And then everyone had 2 voting dots to vote on the challenges we considered to be the most pertinent to solve. Two of our clients also had 2 voting dots in different color to vote on the challenges.

02 /  HWMs

Focusing on the voted and prioritized problems, we rewrote each one as a standardized challenge. We rephrased our post-it in “How Might We” format, which allows us to make it solvable and standardize the way the challenges are written. Here’s some examples that problem might be written into a more general challenge:

  • HMW increase awareness of CHID?
  • HMW stronger CHID’s public image?
  • HMW build CHID community?
  • HMW showcase student work?
    • ...

03 /  Effort & impact scale

An Impact Effort Matrix is a tool that helps us decide which solution we should pursue from a list of possible solutions. By using an Impact Effort Matrix, we is able to visually prioritize solutions based on ease of implementation.

In order to solve those questions, we divided into three teams: swag team, website team and video team. We came up with different solutions based on the chosen areas. Our website team is focusing onredesigning CHID’s website. The other two teams work on creating CHID’s swags and videos.

04 /  Card sorting for navigation

05 /  Vote for the homepage content elements

Critical (Had two or three votes) elements:

  • Visual representation of “Question, Critique, Create”
  • News & Events Section
  • Slider with featured students/staff
  • Study Abroad Section
  • Why CHID? (ft. Video series)
  • Highlighted CHID Courses
  • “Support Us” button either on the top nav or somewhere near it

Optional (Had only one vote) elements:

  • Intro/Welcome paragraph
  • Word Cloud
  • “If you can dream it…”
  • Student + Staff summary of what CHID means to them
  • Photo gallery of CHID community
